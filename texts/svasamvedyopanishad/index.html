The Upanishads represent the pinnacle of Vedic philosophy, offering profound insights into the nature of reality and consciousness. प्रथमः श्लोकः • Verse 1

Sanskrit Original

सर्वेषां प्राणिबुद्बुदानां निरञ्जनाव्यक्तामृतनिधौ विलयविलासः स्थितिर्विजृम्भते । तेषामेव पुनर्भवनं नो इहास्ति । स यथा मृत्पिण्डे घटानां तन्तौ पटानां तथैवेति भवति । वस्तुतो नोपादानमत एव नोपादेयमत एव न निमित्तमत एव न विद्या न पुराणं नो वेदा नेतिहासा इति न जगदिति न ब्रह्मा नो विष्णुः नाथ रुद्रो नेश्वरो न बिन्दुः नो कलेति अग्रे मध्येऽवसाने सर्वं यथावस्थितं यथावस्थितज्ञानं तेषां नो भवत्यागमपुराणेतिहासधर्मशास्त्रेषु धृताभिमानास्ते । यत्तानि तु मुग्धतरमुनिशब्दवाच्यैः जीवबुद्बुदैः रचितानीति भवन्ति । तत्र प्रामाण्यं तादृशानामेव । ते त्वज्ञानेनावृताः सयत्नेन गर्भास्तदप्येष श्लोको भवति । तदत्र श्लोको भवति ॥

English Translation

In the pure, undifferentiated, imperishable treasure of existence, the manifestation and dissolution of all living beings appear like bubbles. For them, there is no rebirth here.

As pots arise from a lump of clay and fabrics from threads, so does all come to be. In reality, there is neither cause nor effect, neither instrumental nor material cause. There is no knowledge, no ancient lore, no Vedas, no history. The world does not exist; Brahma, Vishnu, Lord Rudra, Ishvara, the subtle point (Bindu), or the limitation (Kala) — none of these exist. Before, during, and after, everything remains as it is, with knowledge of its own existence.

Those caught up in dogmatic beliefs concerning Agamas, Puranas, Itihasas, and Dharma Shastras are in truth created by the bubble-like beings called ignorant sages. The authority belongs only to such beings. Covered by ignorance, they become engrossed in efforts like embryos. This is expressed in this verse.

Key Concepts: Brahma (The Absolute Reality) • Vishnu (The Sustainer) • Agamas (Traditional scriptures) • Rebirth • Illusion (Maya)

द्वितीयः श्लोकः • Verse 2

Sanskrit Original

इह तेनाप्यज्ञानेन नो किञ्चित् । अथ यथावस्थितज्ञानेन किञ्चित् नेति यदस्ति तदस्ति यन्नास्ति नास्ति तत् । कालकर्मात्मकमिदं स्वभावात्मकं चेति । न सुकृतं नो दुष्कृतम् । अत एव सुमेरुदातारो गोदातारो वा गोघ्नैः ब्राह्मणघ्नैः सुरापानैः पश्यतोहरैः परोक्षहरैर्वा गुरुपापनिष्ठैः सर्वपापनिष्ठैः समानास्त एते । तैश्च न गौः न ब्राह्मणः न सुरा न पश्यतोहरः न परोक्षहरः न गुरुपापानि न लघुपापानि मत एव तन्निष्ठाः मत एव न निर्वाणं नो निरय इति तदप्येष श्लोको भवति ॥

English Translation

By that ignorance, nothing is known. But through the knowledge of things as they are, something is known. That which exists, exists; that which does not exist, does not exist.

All this is composed of Kala (Time), Karma (Action), and inherent nature. There is neither virtuous deed (Sukṛta) nor sinful deed (Duṣkṛta). Therefore, those who give Mount Meru in charity or who give cows in charity are equal to those who kill cows, kill Brahmins, drink wine, steal things before one's eyes or secretly, and those who are established in the sins against their gurus and in all sins.

For them, there is no cow, no Brahmin, no wine, no open stealing, no secret stealing, no grave sins, no minor sins. They are established in that alone. For them, there is neither liberation (Nirvana) nor hell. This is expressed in this verse.

Key Concepts: Kala (Time) • Karma (Action) • Sukṛta (Virtue) • Duṣkṛta (Sin) • Nirvana (Liberation)

तृतीयः श्लोकः • Verse 3

Sanskrit Original

तत्त्वज्ञानं गुहायां निविष्टमज्ञानिकृतमार्गं सुष्ठु वदन्ति । ते तत्र साभिमाना वर्तन्ते । पुष्पितवचनेन मोहितास्ते भवन्ति । स यथातुरा भिषग्ग्रहणकाले बाला अपथ्याहितगुडादिना जनन्या वञ्चिता इति नानादेवता गुरुकर्मतीर्थनिष्ठाश्च ते भवन्ति । केचिद्वयं वैदिका इति वदन्ति । नान्येऽस्मभ्यम् । केचिद्वयं सर्वशास्त्रज्ञा इति । केचिद्वयं देवानुग्रहवन्तः । केचिद्वयं स्वप्ने उपास्यदेवताभाषिणः । केचिद्वयं देवा इति । केचिद्वयं श्रीमद्रमारमणनलिनभृङ्गा इति । केचित्तु नृत्यन्तु । केचित्तु मूर्खा वयं परमभक्ता इति वदन्तो रुदन्ति पतन्ति च । ये केचनैते ते सर्वेऽप्यज्ञानिनः । ये तु ज्ञानिनो भवन्ति ये तत्त्वज्ञानिनश्च तैस्तेषां को विशेषः । मत एव केषाञ्चित्कैश्चिद्भेदः । मत एव यत्र विरिञ्चिविष्णुरुद्रा ईश्वरश्च गच्छन्ति तत्रैव श्वानो गर्दभाः मार्जाराः कृमयश्च मत एव न श्वानगर्दभौ न मार्जारः न कृमिः नौत्तमाः न मध्यमाः न जघन्याः । तदप्येष श्लोको भवति ॥

English Translation

The knowledge of truth is well-proclaimed as established in the cave (of the heart) by the ignorant through various paths. They dwell there with pride. They become deluded by flowery speech.

Just as children are deceived by their mothers with inappropriate sweets at the time of taking medicine, so they become attached to various deities, guru's actions, and pilgrimage places.

Some say: "We follow the Vedas; no others are equal to us." Some say: "We know all scriptures." Some say: "We have the grace of the divine." Some say: "In dreams, we converse with our worshipful deities." Some say: "We are divine beings." Some say: "We are like humming bees around the lotus feet of the glorious Rama." Some dance around. Some say: "We ignorant ones are supreme devotees," and while saying so, they cry and fall down.

All these are ignorant. But those who are knowers of truth (Jñānins) and knowers of reality (Tattvajñānins), what difference is there? Due to partiality alone there is some difference. Where Brahma, Vishnu, and Rudra merge, right there also go the dogs, asses, cats, and worms. Thus, there is no dog, no ass, no cat, no worm, no superior, no medium, no inferior. This is expressed in this verse.

Key Concepts: Tattvajñānins (Knowers of Truth) • Jñānins (The Wise) • Brahma • Vishnu • Rudra (Destroyer)

चतुर्थः श्लोकः • Verse 4

Sanskrit Original

न तच्छब्दः न किंशब्दः न सर्वे शब्दाः न माता नो पिता न बन्धुः न भार्या न पुत्रो न मित्रं नो सर्वे तथापि साधकैरात्मस्वरूपं वेदितुमिच्छद्भिर्जीवन्मुमुक्षुभिः सन्तः सेव्याः । भार्या पुत्रो गृहं धनं सर्वं तेभ्यो देयम् । कर्माद्वैतं न कार्यं भावाद्वैतं तु कार्यम् । निश्चयेन सर्वाद्वैतं कर्तव्यम् । गुरौ द्वैतमवश्यं कार्यम् । यतो न तस्मादन्यत् । येन सर्वमिदं प्रकाशितम् । कोऽन्यः तस्मात्परः । स जीवन्मुक्तो भवति स जीवन्मुक्तो भवति । य एवं वेद । य एवं वेद ॥

English Translation

There is no word "that," nor "what," nor any words at all. There is no mother, no father, no relative, no wife, no son, no friend—none at all.

Yet, for the Sadhakas (spiritual aspirants) who wish to know the nature of the Self while living as seekers of liberation (Mumukṣus), they should be served. Wife, son, home, wealth—all should be given to them.

Karma Advaita (action without duality) should not be performed, but Bhāva Advaita (non-duality in consciousness) should be performed. With certainty, Sarvādvaita (complete non-duality) should be practiced. Towards the guru, duality is to be maintained, for there is nothing apart from him by whom all this is illumined.

Who other than him is supreme? He becomes liberated while living (Jīvanmukta). He becomes liberated while living. He who knows thus. He who knows thus.

Key Concepts: Sadhakas (Spiritual Seekers) • Mumukṣus (Liberation Seekers) • Karma Advaita (Non-dual Action) • Bhāva Advaita (Non-dual Consciousness) • Sarvādvaita (Universal Non-duality) • Jīvanmukta (Liberated While Living)

इति स्वसंवेद्योपनिषत् सम्पूर्णा

Thus concludes the Svasaṃvedyopaniṣad

"The knower of Truth crosses over sorrow; the knower of Truth crosses over sin; liberated from the knots of the heart, the knower becomes immortal"
