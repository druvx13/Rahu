ईशावास्योपनिषद्

The Song of Divine Union

Sacred Vedic Wisdom for Eternal Realization

Invocation

ॐ पूर्णमदः पूर्णमिदं पूर्णात्पूर्णमुदच्यते।
पूर्णस्य पूर्णमादाय पूर्णमेवावशिष्यते॥
ॐ शान्तिः शान्तिः शान्तिः ॥

Translation

Om. That is full; this is full. This fullness has been projected from that fullness.
When this fullness merges in that fullness, all that remains is fullness.
Om. Peace! Peace! Peace!

१

Verse 1

ईशावास्यमिदं सर्वं यत्किञ्च जगत्यां जगत् ।
तेन त्यक्तेन भुञ्जीथा मा गृधः कस्य स्विद्धनम् ॥ १॥

All this-whatever exists in this changing universe-should be covered by the Lord.
Protect the Self by renunciation. Lust not after any man's wealth.

२

Verse 2

यस्य वा अर्थे कामानां त्यागो निर्मितो महान् ।
धनेन्द्रियार्थैर्मुच्येत तं मृत्युमृत्युराप्नुयात् ॥ २॥

If a man wishes to live a hundred years on this earth, he should live performing action.
For you, who cherish such a desire and regard yourself as a man,
There is no other way by which you can keep work from clinging to you.

३

Verse 3

अन्धं तमः प्रविशन्ति येऽविद्यां उपासते ।
ततो भूय इव ते तमो यस्ते विद्यामुपासते ॥ ३॥

Verily, those worlds of the asuras are enveloped in blind darkness;
And thereto they all repair after death who are slayers of Atman.

४

Verse 4

अप्राणो ह्यमनाः शुभ्रो निर्लेपोऽशब्द एव सः ।
निरञ्जनः पर्यगात्साक्षातात्मा मुख्यः प्रतिष्ठितः ॥ ४॥

That non-dual Atman, though never stirring, is swifter than the mind.
The senses cannot reach It, for It moves ever in front.
Though standing still, It overtakes others who are running.
Because of Atman, Vayu, the World Soul apportions the activities of all.

५

Verse 5

दूरे तद्विपरीते च यत्स्थाणुः परिभ्रमत् ।
सत्त्वमेकं बहुधा यः करोति तं विद्यात्पुरुषं महान्तम् ॥ ५॥

It moves and moves not; It is far and likewise near.
It is inside all this and It is outside all this.

६

Verse 6

यस्मिन् सर्वाणि भूतानि आत्मैवाभूद्विजानतः ।
तत्र को मोहः कः शोक एकत्वमनुपश्यतः ॥ ६॥

The wise man beholds all beings in the Self and the Self in all beings;
For that reason he does not hate anyone.

७

Verse 7

तदेजति तन्नेजति तद्दूरे तद्विपरीते च ।
तद्यद्वास्तु सर्वस्य तद्वै तल्लोके वदन्ति च ॥ ७॥

To the seer, all things have verily become the Self:
What delusion, what sorrow, can there be for him who beholds that oneness?

८

Verse 8

एको हि भगवान् सर्वगतः सर्वत्र व्याप्तः ।
तस्माद्विश्वमिदं तस्मिन्नन्तर्बहिश्च तिष्ठति ॥ ८॥

It is He who pervades all-He who is bright and bodiless,
Without scar or sinews, pure and by evil unpierced;
Who is the Seer, omniscient, transcendent and uncreated.
He has duly allotted to the eternal World-Creators their respective duties.

९

Verse 9

अन्धं तमः प्रविशन्ति ये विद्यामुपासते ।
ततो भूय इव ते तमो यस्ते विद्यामुपासते ॥ ९॥

Into a blind darkness they enter who are devoted to ignorance (rituals);
But into a greater darkness they enter who engage in knowledge of a deity alone.

१०

Verse 10

एकं विद्यामिति चाहुरेकं विद्यामिति चाहुः ।
विद्यां चाविद्यां च यस्तद्वेदोभयं सह ॥ १०॥

One thing, they say, is obtained from knowledge; another, they say, from ignorance.
Thus we have heard from the wise who have taught us this.

११

Verse 11

स विद्वानमृतं प्रेति नान्यः पन्था अयनाय विद्यते ॥ ११॥

He who is aware that both knowledge and ignorance should be pursued together,
Overcomes death through ignorance and obtains immortality through knowledge.

१२

Verse 12

अन्धं तमः प्रविशन्ति ये विद्यामुपासते ।
ततो भूय इव ते तमो यस्ते विद्यामुपासते ॥ १२॥

Into a blind darkness they enter who worship only the unmanifested prakriti;
But into a greater darkness they enter who worship the manifested Hiranyagarbha.

१३

Verse 13

एकं विद्यामिति चाहुरेकं विद्यामिति चाहुः ।
विद्यां चाविद्यां च यस्तद्वेदोभयं सह ॥ १३॥

One thing, they say, is obtained from the worship of the manifested;
Another, they say, from the worship of the unmanifested.
Thus we have heard from the wise who taught us this.

१४

Verse 14

स विद्वानमृतं प्रेति नान्यः पन्था अयनाय विद्यते ॥ १४॥

He who knows that both the unmanifested prakriti and the manifested Hiranyagarbha
Should be worshipped together, overcomes death by the worship of Hiranyagarbha
And obtains immortality through devotion to prakriti.

१५

Verse 15

अन्धेनैव तमः प्रविशन्ति ये विद्यामुपासते ।
ततो भूय इव ते तमो यस्ते विद्यामुपासते ॥ १५॥

The door of the Truth is covered by a golden disc.
Open it, O Nourisher! Remove it so that I who have been worshipping the Truth may behold It.

१६

Verse 16

त्वमेवाहमस्मि योऽन्यो ऽहं त्वत्त्वात्मा न पृथग्विधः ।
एतावानस्य महिमा अथामी लोका जयन्ति ते ॥ १६॥

O Nourisher, lone Traveller of the sky! Controller! O Sun, Offspring of Prajapati!
Gather Your rays; withdraw Your light.
I would see, through Your grace, that form of Yours which is the fairest.
I am indeed He, that Purusha, who dwells there.

१७

Verse 17

अन्धं तमः प्रविशन्ति ये विद्यामुपासते ।
ततो भूय इव ते तमो यस्ते विद्यामुपासते ॥ १७॥

Now may my breath return to the all-pervading, immortal Prana!
May this body be burnt to ashes! Om.
O mind, remember, remember all that I have done.

१८

Verse 18

एतावानस्य महिमा अथामी लोका जयन्ति ते ।
एतावान्यस्य महिमा अथामी लोका जयन्ति ते ॥ १८॥

O Fire, lead us by the good path for the enjoyment of the fruit of our action.
You know, O god, all our deeds.
Destroy our sin of deceit. We offer, by words, our salutations to you.

॥ इत्येषा ईशावास्योपनिषद् सम्पूर्णा ॥

Thus concludes the Isa Upanishad - the eternal song of divine union

The Peace Chant

Om. Peace! Peace! Peace!
