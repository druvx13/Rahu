The Supreme Non-Dual Reality

A profound Sanskrit mantra revealing the nature of ultimate reality

Original Sanskrit (Devanagari)

न सृष्टिर्नापि संहारः ।
न बन्धो न च साधकः ।
अजमेतदद्वयं हि परम् ॥

IAST Transliteration

Na sṛṣṭirnāpi saṁhāraḥ ।
Na bandho na ca sādhakaḥ ।
Ajāmetadadvayaṁ hi param ॥

Approximate English Meaning

"There is no creation, nor is there dissolution;
No bondage exists, nor indeed any aspirant;
This unborn, non-dual [reality] is verily the supreme."

Understanding the Mantra

This profound verse points to the ultimate nature of reality beyond all conceptual frameworks. It negates the fundamental concepts that form the basis of our understanding of existence:

  • No creation/dissolution: The universe isn't created nor destroyed in ultimate reality
  • No bondage: The sense of limitation or separation is illusory
  • No aspirant: Even the seeker of truth is part of the illusion

The mantra culminates by pointing to the supreme reality that is unborn (beyond birth/death) and non-dual (beyond all distinctions and separations).
